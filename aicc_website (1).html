Our Story

About AICC

The All India Christian Confederation — established to be the unified, non-partisan voice of Christians across India, standing firmly against injustice in all its forms.

Introduction

Who We Are

AICC is dedicated to fostering unity and defending the rights of Christians across India. We transcend caste divisions to promote an inclusive ethos within the Christian community. AICC addresses injustices, advocates for legal protections, and supports socio-economically disadvantaged Christians.

Through peaceful protests, legal engagement, and public advocacy, we safeguard churches and believers from atrocities. We work towards fair representation in governance, secure cemetery land, and ensure the legal recognition of church properties. Collaborating with civil society and international organizations, AICC amplifies advocacy efforts and promotes Christian perspectives in public discourse and policymaking.

Direction

Vision & Mission

Vision

A united, inclusive, and empowered Christian community in India — free from caste divisions, discrimination, and marginalization — with full and equal participation in the nation's civic, political, and social life.

Mission

To defend the rights of Christians across India through legal advocacy, peaceful campaigns, community support, and strategic partnerships — ensuring justice, representation, and dignity for every believer.

What We Do

Programs & Services

Five pillars of action defining how AICC serves and protects Christians across every corner of India.

⚖️
Legal

Advocacy & Legal Support

AICC provides comprehensive legal assistance to Christians facing persecution, discrimination, or violations of their religious freedom. We engage directly with the judicial system to ensure fair outcomes for victims of anti-Christian violence. Our legal work spans property disputes involving church land, criminal cases of religious persecution, and ensuring existing laws protecting religious minorities are enforced effectively. We also advocate for stronger legislation where gaps exist, working with experienced civil rights attorneys across states.

🤝
Community

Community Support & Upliftment

Recognizing that socio-economic disadvantage disproportionately affects marginalized Christian communities, AICC implements targeted programs to break cycles of poverty and exclusion. Initiatives include scholarship programs for Christian students, vocational training and employment placement, micro-finance support for small businesses, and establishment of community centers offering resources and safe spaces. We work at the grassroots level, partnering with local churches and organizations for sustained impact.

📢
Education

Awareness & Education

Building understanding is essential to building justice. AICC conducts educational programs targeting both the Christian community and broader Indian society. For Christians, we provide know-your-rights workshops, leadership training, and capacity-building programs. For the general public, we work with educational institutions to promote accurate Christian history and engage media for balanced representation of Christian communities and their contributions to Indian society.

✊
Campaigns

Advocacy Campaigns

When dialogue alone is insufficient, AICC organizes peaceful, democratic advocacy campaigns that make the collective voice of Indian Christians heard. We coordinate protests, public demonstrations, media campaigns, and lobbying efforts targeting both state and central government. Campaigns have focused on securing cemetery land, opposing unconstitutional interference in church affairs, and pushing for fair representation in government institutions — always within lawful, democratic means.

📋
Monitoring

Documentation & Monitoring

AICC maintains a rigorous system for monitoring, documenting, and reporting incidents of discrimination and violence against Christians across India. This database serves as evidence for legal cases, policy advocacy, and international reporting. We collaborate with international human rights organizations to amplify findings globally, creating accountability. Our monitoring mechanism also tracks implementation of existing laws protecting religious minorities, ensuring authorities fulfill their legal obligations.
